Journal of Proteins & Proteomics, Vol 5, No 4 (2014)

ENHANCED NITRILASE PRODUCTION BY RHODOCOCCUS PYRIDINIVORANS SN2 USING AN OPTIMIZED BIPHASIC FERMENTATION APPROACH AND ITS USE FOR NICOTINIC ACID SYNTHESIS

Karthikeya Kameswaran, Richi V Mahajan, Rajendra Kumar Saxena

Abstract


In the present study, nitrilase activity of Rhodococcus pyridinivorans SN2 was optimized by a combinationof response surface methodology and biphasic fed batch strategy. The optimized conditions were 15 g l-1 of yeastextract and 6 g l-1 of benzonitrile (in feeds of 1 g l-1), followed by a feed of 10 g l-1 fructose and 4 g l-1?-caprolactamleading to a biomass yield of 101 gdcw l-1 in 48 h with nitrilase activity of 15020 Ugdcw-1 and a nitrilase productivityof around 1500 U l-1. This represented a 16 fold increase in nitrilase productivity from 90 U l-1 under unoptimized conditions. Whole cells (2 gdcw) of Rhodococcus pyridinivorans SN2 when immobilized in calcium alginate beadscompletely hydrolysed 10.5 g 3-cyanopyridine (~0.1 mol) to nicotinic acid in 6.5 hours using a fed batch reactionat a scale of 100 ml.

Keywords: Nitrilase; nicotinic acid; Rhodococcus; RSM; biphasic
